## Quick Answer

**"Beli vs Bikin Martabak Pecenongan" flopped because:**
1. **Duration was too short** (40s vs 67s for high performers) — not enough time to build hook
2. **Speech percentage too low** (57% vs 70% for high performers) — insufficient narration
3. **Food topic mismatch** — street food items (martabak, soto, bakso) underperform; home cooking (kaldu, telur, resep) overperform
4. **No cluster alignment** — post fell outside Cluster 134 (Cooking/Collaboration) territory
5. **Format wrong for the topic** — comparison/review angle works better for premium/specialty items, NOT commodity street foods

This is NOT a failure of the "beli vs bikin" format itself—it's a failure of topic + execution misalignment.

## Format Analysis: Why Duration & Narration Matter

### Low Performer Profile (The Failed Beli Post)

**Typical Failed Structure:**
1. **Hook (2s):** "Beli vs bikin martabak pecenongan"
2. **Clip (5s):** Quick pan to bought martabak
3. **DIY start (25s):** Very fast DIY process, minimal explanation
4. **Total: ~40s**
5. **Narration: 57%** — Too much on-screen text, not enough explanation

**Problem:** At 40s with low speech, there's no time to:
- Build curiosity about the difference
- Explain WHY DIY is better (cost? freshness? skill?)
- Show the process clearly
- Create emotional investment

### High Performer Profile (The Successful Beli Post)

**Typical Successful Structure (Kaldu Ayam Template):**
1. **Hook (3s):** "Karena ini cara tergampang untuk bikin kaldu ayam kampung..." + why it matters
2. **Context (5s):** Why people need this (health, nutrients for kids)
3. **Step-by-step (60s):** Show 4-5 steps with heavy narration
   - "Simpen ayam kampung, saking kayaknya ini..."
   - "Masukkan jahe, karena aromanya akan..."
   - "Tunggu 30 menit sampai warna berubah jadi..."
4. **Total: 84s**
5. **Narration: 100%** — Full narration explains each step and builds credibility

**Advantage:** Viewers understand:
- Why they should make it (health/cost/freshness)
- Exactly how to do it (step-by-step)
- Why DIY > buying pre-made
- Emotional payoff (I can do this for my family)

## Recommendation: When to Use Beli vs Bikin Frame

### ✅ DO Use "Beli vs Bikin" For:

1. **Skill-based items** (kaldu, bone broth, fermented foods, specialty sauces)
   - Example brief: "Kaldu ayam gourmet — beli di mall Rp150K, bikin sendiri Rp45K + lebih segar"

2. **Health-focused foods** (kombucha, kefir, green juice)
   - Example brief: "Beli minuman kesehatan mahal, bikin dengan 3 bahan doang"

3. **Surprise DIY hacks** (foods people didn't know could be homemade)
   - Example brief: "Udah tahu rice noodles bisa bikin sendiri? Ga sempet beli, bikin aja"

4. **Premium/specialty items** (expensive when bought, accessible when DIY)
   - Example brief: "Espresso pelerin Rp80K vs bikin di rumah Rp5K"

### ❌ DON'T Use "Beli vs Bikin" For:

1. **Commodity street foods** (soto, bakso, martabak, gudeg)
   - Why: Everyone already knows they exist. Comparison adds no new info.

2. **Foods requiring specialized equipment**
   - Why: Can't teach DIY in 60-90s without equipment shots. Lowers perceived accessibility.

3. **Foods where DIY isn't actually cheaper**
   - Why: No cost motivation. Frame becomes "why would I make this at home?"

4. **Foods that don't benefit from narration-heavy format**
   - Why: Won't hit Cluster 134's speech% signal. Performance drops.

## Final Verdict: The Martabak Pecenongan Case

If rivaldo.philip (or any creator) wanted to create "Beli vs Bikin Martabak Pecenongan" content:

**The post would likely fail because:**

1. ❌ Martabak is a commodity street food, not a skill. People don't watch to LEARN; they watch to browse.
2. ❌ DIY martabak requires griddle + specialty ingredients. Not accessible DIY. Loses "I can do this" factor.
3. ❌ Short duration (40s) + low speech (50-60%) → won't trigger Cluster 134 algorithmic boost
4. ❌ No comparison value — street martabak quality is so variable that "beli vs bikin" doesn't matter; it's about WHICH vendor

**To make it work:**

**Reframe as:** "3 Minute Martabak Rumahan (Tanpa Peli) + Isi 5 Varian"
- Focus: DIY hack angle, not beli-vs-bikin comparison
- Duration: 70-85s to show technique
- Speech: 80%+ to explain each step
- Target: Cluster 134 (learning/DIY)
- Hook: Speed + variety, not cost comparison

This would succeed because it's now **skill-focused DIY**, not a **commodity comparison**.
